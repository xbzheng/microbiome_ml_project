Microbiome Profiling

Risk Predictor for Colorectal Cancer.

Authors

  • Xiaobin (Ivan) Zheng, PhD. Data Engineering Lead and Data Scientist at Insight Data Science
  • Zhongyun (Julie) Huang, PhD. Insight Health Data Science Fellow (Winter 2017), and currently works as a Principal Scientist at Roche Sequencing Solutions


Summary

Colorectal cancer is the 2nd most common cancer with over 200,000 new cases each year in the US. The Fecal Occult Test is widely used to screen for colorectal cancer with an average test accuracy of 45%. Here we developed a machine learning method (Logistics Regression Model with L1 Regularization) to model metagenomics sequences of fecal samples for the risk prediction of colorectal cancer. The final model achieved performance accuracy of 73 % and identified important taxonomic markers that distinguished colorectal cancer patients from the healthy individual.


Our machine learning method is freely available to the public to facilitate research collaboration environment in the Health Data Science field.
